## Strategic Context

We are a payments infrastructure company serving SMBs and marketplaces across Europe. In Q4 2025 we are focused on regulatory compliance for the new PSD3 requirements, expanding into two new markets (Poland and the Netherlands), and reducing fraud losses which have grown as transaction volume scaled.

## Company Objectives

### CO-1: Achieve and maintain PSD3 compliance ahead of the January deadline

**Objective**: Complete all technical and operational changes required by PSD3 so we are compliant before the regulatory deadline and retain our operating licences in all active markets.

**Key Results**
1. All 14 PSD3 technical requirements implemented and signed off by legal by 1 December
2. Complete an independent compliance audit with zero critical findings by 15 December
3. Staff training completion rate for PSD3 policy changes reaches 100% by 30 November

---

### CO-2: Launch in Poland and the Netherlands and reach initial transaction targets

**Objective**: Go live in two new markets and validate that our product and operations can scale to them within the quarter.

**Key Results**
1. Process first live transaction in Poland by end of October and in the Netherlands by end of November
2. Reach €2M cumulative transaction volume across both new markets by end of Q4
3. Onboard at least 15 paying merchants in each new market by end of Q4

---

### CO-3: Reduce fraud losses as a percentage of transaction volume

**Objective**: Bring fraud losses back within acceptable bounds as transaction volume grows, without materially increasing false positive rates that harm legitimate merchants.

**Key Results**
1. Reduce fraud losses from 0.18% to 0.09% of total transaction volume by end of Q4
2. Keep false positive rate (legitimate transactions declined) below 0.4%
3. Reduce mean time to detect and block a new fraud pattern from 72 hours to 18 hours

---

## Business Context Notes

- PSD3 work is the top company priority; all teams should expect to absorb some compliance-related tasks regardless of their primary focus
- The Poland and Netherlands launches are being led by the Growth team; Engineering and Platform teams should treat their launch dependencies as P1 items
- Fraud tooling improvements are owned by the Risk team but depend on data pipeline work from the Platform team — this dependency should be captured in any Platform OKRs that contribute to CO-3
- Year-end hiring freeze goes into effect 1 December; headcount plans must be finalised before then
