# Sandbagging in OKRs

Source: https://www.whatmatters.com/faqs/sandbagging-under-promise-deliver-okrs

## What it is

Sandbagging means setting targets based only on what the team can accomplish without changing current practices — under-promising to guarantee over-delivery. It feels safe but undermines the purpose of OKRs.

## Why it's harmful

When goals aren't genuinely ambitious, teams stop innovating. Sandbagged OKRs produce incremental output, not competitive differentiation. The result: underperforming teams and underwhelmed customers.

## Red flags

- The team consistently meets all committed and aspirational goals without fully consuming available resources
- Targets are only slightly above the current baseline — incremental rather than transformational
- Product releases deliver only basic, expected features rather than meaningful differentiation
- Employee goals are disconnected from executive strategy

## How to prevent it

- **Committed OKRs** should credibly consume most of the team's available capacity. If the team has obvious slack, the target is too low.
- **Aspirational OKRs** should feel uncomfortable and potentially unattainable. The combined load of committed + aspirational OKRs should exceed what the team can comfortably deliver.
- Decouple compensation from OKR scores so people are not incentivised to sandbag for safety.
- Ask of every aspirational OKR: "Does this meet or exceed what our customers are actually asking for?"
