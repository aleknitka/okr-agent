# KPIs vs OKRs

Source: https://www.whatmatters.com/okrs-explained/kpis-and-okrs

## The distinction

KPIs are periodic measurements that track how an organisation is doing — a health dashboard covering revenue, uptime, customer satisfaction, and similar standing metrics. They are always on.

OKRs are transformational goals focused on what matters most right now. They have a cycle, a specific target, and an end date. "OKRs are KPIs with soul because they give direction and purpose."

OKRs do not replace KPIs. They complement them.

## When to promote a KPI to a key result

A standing KPI becomes a key result when it is elevated from routine tracking to an active growth target. Example: email signups are normally a KPI on the marketing dashboard. If the objective is "reach a wider audience", email signups with a target of +50% by end of quarter becomes a KR.

The test: Is this metric something the team is actively trying to move this cycle, with dedicated resources and a specific target? If yes, it belongs in an OKR. If it's just being monitored, it stays as a KPI.

## Red flag

Key results that are indistinguishable from existing dashboard metrics — no stretch, no deadline, no ownership beyond "keep an eye on it" — are KPIs masquerading as OKRs. Flag these for replacement with genuine outcome targets.
